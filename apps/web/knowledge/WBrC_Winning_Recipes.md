# World Brewers Cup (WBrC) Winning Recipes

Here are the winning recipes from recent World Brewers Cup champions:

## 2024: Martin Wölfl (Austria)
**Focus:** Sweetness and clarity

*   **Coffee:** 17g (Panama Geisha, Finca Maya, processed by Lost Origin)
*   **Water:** 270ml at 93°C (Customized with Apax Lab minerals)
*   **Grind:** Medium (approx. 600–700 microns; 21–25 clicks on Comandante C40)
*   **Dripper:** Orea V4 with Sibarist FAST filters
*   **Method:**
    1.  **0:00:** Pour 60ml (Bloom). Use a Melodrip or shower screen for gentle dispersion.
    2.  **0:40:** Pour 60ml (Total 120ml).
    3.  **1:20:** Pour 50ml (Total 170ml).
    4.  **2:00:** Pour 100ml (Total 270ml).
*   **Total Time:** 2:20 – 2:25
*   **The "Secret":** Martin used a "negotiator" tool to fit the filter perfectly to the dripper walls (eliminating bypass) and a Melodrip to minimize agitation during the later pours.

## 2023: Carlos Medina (Chile)
**Focus:** High replicability and consistent intervals

*   **Coffee:** 15.5g (Natural Sidra, Café Granja La Esperanza, Colombia)
*   **Water:** 250ml at 91°C (65ppm, equal parts Calcium and Magnesium)
*   **Dripper:** Origami Air S (Polycarbonate) with conical filters
*   **Method:**
    *   **5 Pours:** Pour 50ml every 30 seconds (0:00, 0:30, 1:00, 1:30, 2:00).
*   **Total Time:** 2:40 – 2:45
*   **The "Secret":** He used the Origami Air (plastic) specifically for its thermal stability and paired it with the Origami Sensory Cup to enhance the aromatic experience for the judges.

## 2022: Shih Yuan Hsu (Sherry) (Taiwan)
**Focus:** Temperature stepping to target specific flavor compounds

*   **Coffee:** 14g (Natural Carbonic Maceration Geisha, Finca Mikava, Colombia)
*   **Water:** 200ml total
*   **Dripper:** Orea V3 (Flat bottom) with Kalita 185 filters
*   **Method:**
    1.  **1st Pour (0:00):** 50ml at **70°C**. This lower temperature targets bright, malic acidity.
    2.  **2nd Pour (0:30):** 50ml at **95°C**. The jump in temperature extracts sweetness and body.
    3.  **3rd Pour (1:00):** 50ml at 95°C.
    4.  **4th Pour (1:30):** 50ml at 95°C.
*   **Total Time:** 2:30 – 3:00
*   **The "Secret":** She used a dual-grind strategy, mixing 75% coarse grounds (1000 microns) for acidity and 25% finer grounds (800 microns) for sweetness and texture.
